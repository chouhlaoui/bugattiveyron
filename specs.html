Bugatti Veyron Motor

The heart and soul of the VEYRON is its 8-liter W16 engine which, with its four turbochargers, initially produces 1,001 horsepower, and in later models an astounding 1,200 horsepower. Measuring 710 mm in length, the 16-cylinder engine is no more bulky than a conventional V12 block, and thanks to its lightweight construction, its weight does not exceed 490 kg. This engine is capable of operating under continuous full load without limitation — something that racing engines, for example, are unable to achieve. It owes its compact dimensions to the unique W configuration of its cylinder banks. Two VR8 blocks, whose cylinder banks are set at a 15-degree angle, are mounted within a single crankcase to form the engine. These two eight-cylinder blocks are positioned at 90 degrees to each other and are boosted by four turbochargers.

Bugatti Veyron Gearbox

The VEYRON was equipped with the fastest gear-shifting system in the world: the DSG dual-clutch transmission. BUGATTI was the first manufacturer to use a seven-speed DSG gearbox. This transmission, specially designed for the new sports car, performs a task that any other automotive gearbox would fail to handle: it ensures the transfer of engine torque of up to 1,500 Nm. At the time of its development, the VEYRON’s DSG dual-clutch transmission combined— with previously unmatched perfection — the dynamic advantages of a manual gearbox with the comfort of an automatic. No other manufacturer possessed comparable technology at the time.

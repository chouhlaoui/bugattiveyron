Factory — Bugatti Veyron

Production chain — Bugatti Veyron

Bugatti resumed its production in September 2005 to build the Bugatti Veyron 16.4 in Molsheim, on the brand’s historic site. To achieve this, the Volkswagen Group – which, thanks in particular to Ferdinand Piëch, made the revival of the French manufacturer possible – had a new, human-sized, oval-shaped factory built, known as “the Atelier.”

Bugatti, Where the Automobile Becomes Art

Bugatti embodies a unique vision of the automobile, standing at the crossroads of art, engineering, and performance. More than a car manufacturer, it is a myth shaped by rolling masterpieces, bold innovations, human drama, and spectacular rebirths.

Ettore Bugatti, the Artist‑Engineer

Founded in 1909 in Molsheim by Ettore Bugatti, who came from a family of artists, the brand was built on a clear philosophy: a car must be as beautiful as it is efficient. Every mechanical component was designed as a work of art, giving Bugatti its unmistakable identity.

The Type 35, a Racing Legend

Unveiled in 1924, the Type 35 became the most successful racing car in history. Combining lightness, reliability, technical innovation, and elegance, it perfectly represents Ettore Bugatti’s ideal: performance elevated by design.

The Royale, Ultimate Excess

In the 1930s, Bugatti pushed luxury to its limits with the Type 41 Royale. Created for Europe’s royal elite, this extraordinary limousine—produced in only six units—remains a symbol of absolute refinement and one of the most valuable cars ever built.

Tragedy and Decline

The death of Jean Bugatti in 1939, designer of the legendary Type 57 Atlantic, marked a brutal turning point. World War II and the death of Ettore Bugatti in 1947 led to the gradual disappearance of the brand, which fell into decades of dormancy.

A Failed Comeback: the EB110

In the early 1990s, Bugatti briefly returned with the EB110, a technologically revolutionary supercar. Despite its exceptional performance, an unfavorable economic context caused the project to fail, and the brand once again faded away.

Volkswagen and the Rebirth of the Myth

At the end of the 1990s, the Volkswagen Group revived Bugatti with a clear ambition: to make it the ultimate showcase of automotive technology. Launched in 2005, the Veyron 16.4 pushed all limits by combining extreme power, record-breaking speed, and limousine-level comfort, restoring Bugatti to the pinnacle of global prestige.
